The challenge

Marketing was filling the funnel, not closing it

At Scribenote, marketing was treated as a top-of-funnel function — it brought people in, but converting them to paid sat with product and the rest of the team. Meanwhile, thousands of veterinary professionals had signed up for the free plan and gone quiet. They already knew the product. They'd just never had a reason to come back.

I wanted to prove something specific: that marketing could turn that dormant base into revenue — directly, and measurably.

The approach

One offer, two audiences, a dozen behavioral segments

I split the dormant base into two groups — users who signed up and never returned, and free-plan users who were active but had never upgraded — and gave both the same hook: an extended four-week Pro trial, double our usual length.

The moment someone opted in, I worked with our data engineer to track their every move in a Metabase dashboard, then built roughly a dozen behavioral sub-segments around what each person had or hadn't done in the product. From there, nobody got a generic email. People got the next step for exactly where they were.

Mid-campaign

I stopped to ask what was actually landing

Halfway through the trial, I surveyed users to learn what they liked, what they didn't, and what was quietly holding them back from upgrading. Their answers reshaped the back half of the campaign's content — and gave the whole team a sharper read on how trial users really think about the product.

Removing friction

The "upgrade" email couldn't work — so I built the path

When it came time to ask for the sale, the one-click path didn't exist: upgrading from an email meant hunting through the app. So I partnered with our front-end engineer to build a deep link straight to Scribenote's Stripe upgrade page. The final email could finally do its job — intent to paid, in a single tap.

Making it last

A campaign that runs once isn't a growth lever

So I turned the entire content-creation process into a repeatable Claude skill, so the campaign can run every quarter without being rebuilt from scratch. Scribenote now has an automated reactivation motion it can trigger on a schedule — a durable growth lever, not a one-off.
